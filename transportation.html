Transportation

Almost all visitors arrive to Taniti by air, though some arrive on a small cruise ship that docks in Yellow Leaf Bay for one night per week. Taniti is served by a small airport that can accommodate small jets and propeller planes. We also have plenty of ground transportation options to keep your trip moving!

Airport

Almost all visitors arrive to Taniti by air. We are served by a small airport that can accommodate small jets and propeller planes, but we are in the process of expanding the airport so larger jets will be able to land on the island within the next few years.

Cruise Ship

Visitors that prefer to travel by ship can take advantage of our small cruise ship that docks in Yellow Leaf Bay for one night per week. With quick and easy access to ground transportation, you are sure to start off your adventure on the right foot!

Ground

Public buses serve Taniti City and run from 5 a.m. to 11 p.m. every day. Private buses serve the rest of the island. Taxis are available in Taniti City, and rental cars can be rented from a local rental agency near the airport. Bikes and helmets are available to rent from several vendors (helmets are required by law). Taniti City is fairly flat and very walkable. Many tourists stay in the area surrounding Merriton Landing: this area is easy to explore on foot.
